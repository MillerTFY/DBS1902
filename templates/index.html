What Is Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)?

MACD is a trend following momentum indicator which shows the relationship between two moving averages of DBS price. MACD is calculated by subtracting 2 period of exponential moving average (EMA). Normally, investors will use EMA period of 26 and 12 days but not limits to other values. A nine-day EMA of the MACD called the "signal line," is then plotted on top of the MACD line, which can function as a trigger for buy and sell signals. MACD indicators can be interpreted in several ways, but the more common methods are crossovers, divergences, and rapid rises/falls.
